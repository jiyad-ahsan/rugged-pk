# RUGGED.PK Voice Guardrails

Last updated: 2026-03-24
Purpose: keep all writing consistent with the Rugged voice across guides, product, forum, and announcements.

## 1) Core Voice

- Direct, calm, practical.
- Pakistani context first, always.
- Serious without panic.
- Human and grounded, never corporate.
- Preparedness over performance.

## 2) Tone Anchors

- "Field manual" energy, not "marketing brochure" energy.
- "Useful now" beats "impressive sounding".
- We respect fear but do not farm fear.
- We talk like people in Karachi talk to each other when stakes are real.

## 3) Good vs Bad Examples

## Good

- "If comms go down, your family should still have a plan by tonight."
- "This kit is built for apartment life, traffic lockdowns, and short utility failures."
- "Start with water, meds, and a contact tree. Buy gear later."
- "You don't need everything at once. You need a sequence."

## Bad

- "Dominate any survival scenario with military-grade tactical readiness."
- "Be apocalypse-ready with premium gear bundles."
- "Never be helpless again with elite operator equipment."
- "Our revolutionary ecosystem optimizes resilience outcomes."

## 4) Language Rules

- Keep sentences short where possible.
- Use plain words over fancy words.
- Prefer specifics over hype.
- Use local references naturally: Karachi blocks, load-shedding, neighborhood WhatsApp, fuel queues.
- Avoid over-formatting and listicle tone.
- Avoid too many em dashes; keep rhythm conversational.

## 5) Words/Phrases To Avoid

- tacticool
- operator
- battlefield-ready
- apocalypse
- military-grade (unless legally and technically true, usually avoid)
- domination, elite, revolutionary, world-class
- vague motivational language with no instruction

## 6) Words/Phrases To Prefer

- reliable
- practical
- locally sourced
- tested in urban conditions
- shelf-stable
- capability
- rotation
- contingency
- fallback plan

## 7) Channel-Specific Style

## Guides

- Teach like a trusted older sibling.
- Step-by-step structure.
- Explain why each step matters in Pakistani context.
- Focus on action timelines: now, tonight, this week.

## Product Cards

- Lead with capability, not ingredient list.
- Keep claims conservative and honest.
- Show who it is for, duration, and conditions.
- No inflated specs or imported marketing language.

## Forum Announcements

- Clear and brief.
- Respectful moderation tone.
- Set boundaries without sounding robotic.
- Encourage signal, discourage noise.

## Support/Checkout Messages

- Reassuring and explicit.
- No legalese tone.
- State next action clearly.

## 8) Claim Safety Rules (Critical)

- Walkie range claims must reflect dense urban Karachi constraints.
- Solar language must not imply infinite power.
- Kit cards must describe outcomes (calories/day, days, people, liters processed).
- If unsure, under-claim and clarify assumptions.

## 9) Editorial Checklist Before Publish

- Does this sound like a Pakistani team, not imported copy?
- Is this useful in the first 10 seconds?
- Are claims specific and defensible?
- Are we leading with capability where needed?
- Did we avoid panic-bait language?
- Is any sentence trying too hard to "sound cool"?
- Could a first-time reader act on this today?

## 10) Reusable Microcopy Patterns

- "Start here, then build."
- "Use what you already have first."
- "Plan beats panic."
- "Built for city reality."
- "Tested for disruption, not theory."
- "The kit we wish wasn't necessary."
